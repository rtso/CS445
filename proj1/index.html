Project 1: Hybrid Images

Part 1: Hybrid Images

My first hybrid image result is of me and a terracotta warrior from the Pacific Science Center in Seattle. Since there is more texture on the terracotta warrior than on me, I chose to use the high-pass filter on the terracotta warrior and the low-pass filter on me.

My second hybrid image is of me and a caricature that was drawn of me. Since the photos were cropped from the same picture, I had to increase the contrast on my face so that you could more easily see the shape when the photo was blurred. I chose a sigma of 1 and a filter size of 20x20.
I ran a low-pass filter on the photo of me. It removed a lot of the signals in the high frequencies.
I ran a high-pass filter on the caricature drawing, retaining signal in the higher frequencies.

My third hybrid photo is of photos of my friends and I doing outdoor activities.
I say this result is a fail because when you zoom out and the image becomes really small, it's difficult to see what's going on in the image. There's a lot of detail going on in the climbing image and you lose a lot of that detail when you use a low-pass filter on the image. One way we can fix this is by using the kayaking photo with the low-pass filter and the climbing photo with the high-pass filter. Since the climbing photo has more texture, when using a high-pass filter, the texture would be retained and we would be able to distinguish the photo.

Part 2: Image Enhancement

Color Enhancement

I took a photo of Mount Rainier, converted it from RGB to HSV space, and used gamma transformation with gamma = 0.5 to increase the saturation.

Color Shift

I took a photo of a deer and converted it from RGB to Lab space. For the enhanced image on the right of the original image, I shifted the red-green channel to be more red. You can see that the deer's fur is more red. For the enhanced image below the original image, I shifted the yellow-blue channel to be less yellow. You can see that the grass looks less yellow and the photo looks a lot cooler than the original.

Bells and Whistles

Colorized hybrid image

For the colorized hybrid image, I'm using the climbing and kayaking images as before. Keep in mind that the un-colorized hybrid result turned out a failure. Here I show the filtered images, and it looks like the filtered kayaking photo did not retain a lot of of the color. It works better to use color for the low-frequency component of the hybrid image because the color helps us see distinguish shapes in the image that we otherwise would not see. Keeping the color in the images works better in this case.
